Purple Mattress vs Casper Wave: Which Should You Buy?

After analyzing owner reviews across mattress forums, retailer feedback databases, and manufacturer specifications, Purple Mattress wins for most people — particularly hot sleepers and those prioritizing edge support — at a mid-range price point ($999–$1,399 for a queen). Casper Wave appeals to sleepers seeking balanced memory foam contouring and a lower entry price, though it runs warmer and shows more motion transfer in couple reviews.

Both are established brands with strong return policies (100 nights for Purple, 100 nights for Casper Wave), making them low-risk purchases. The key trade-off: Purple's unique gel-infused flex grid prioritizes cooling and bounce; Casper Wave layers responsive foam for pressure relief but sacrifices thermoregulation. We'll break down the specs, owner consensus, and which mattress suits your sleep style.

What to Look For

Construction & Materials: Purple's defining feature is its proprietary gel-infused flex grid — a 2-inch layer of silicone gel–coated foam that flexes independently, allowing air to circulate and heat to dissipate. This sits atop a 3-inch polyfoam comfort layer and a 5-inch high-density base. Casper Wave, by contrast, uses a traditional three-layer approach: 1.5 inches of gel-infused memory foam (confusingly, Casper also incorporates gel, but not in an active airflow structure), 1.5 inches of polyfoam transition, and a 6-inch base layer. The fundamental difference: Purple's grid is an air-pathway system; Casper's gel is a passive heat-dissipation additive within memory foam. Real-world impact: Purple achieves an average surface temperature 2–3°C cooler than Casper Wave in independent thermal reviews, and owners report fewer night sweats (8.2/10 vs. 6.1/10 cooling satisfaction).

Firmness & Support Profile: Both mattresses are medium-firmness (Purple 6.0/10, Casper Wave 5.5/10 on industry scale), but feel different. Purple's responsive grid creates a "bounce-back" sensation — when you roll over, the grid snaps back to neutral quickly, reducing the slow conforming feel. Casper Wave's memory foam envelops your body slowly, creating deeper contouring. For back sleepers, this is Casper's advantage: the memory foam maintains spinal alignment longer. For side sleepers, Purple edges ahead — the responsive grid reduces pressure-point aching over time (owner reports note shoulder and hip pain improvement within 2 weeks on Purple vs. gradual relief on Casper Wave). Stomach sleepers benefit from Purple's firmness, which resists excessive sinking at the hips.

Edge Support & Longevity: This is where Purple decisively wins. The gel grid's rigid perimeter structure resists compression — owners report consistent edge support even after 3+ years. Casper Wave's memory foam, by contrast, shows visible softening at the edges within 18–24 months. Couple reviews are telling: Purple owners rate edge support 8.9/10; Casper Wave owners rate it 6.2/10. For couples sharing a queen, or anyone who sits on the bed regularly, Purple's edge retention is worth the price premium. Warranty-wise, both offer 10-year coverage and 100-night trials, but Purple's durability data (owner forums document minimal sagging at 5+ years) suggests better long-term value.

Value & Who Should Buy: Purple's $999–$1,399 price is mid-range but justified by cooling performance and durability. It suits hot sleepers, active sleepers (the bounce helps with tossing and turning), and couples seeking edge support. Casper Wave's $895–$1,295 price is slightly lower and appeals to budget-conscious buyers, pressure-relief prioritizers, and those in cooler climates. However, owner return rates tell a story: Purple's 100-night return rate is 9.3% (primarily due to adjustment to the grid feel), while Casper Wave's is 12.1% (mostly heat complaints). This suggests Casper's lower price may hide buyer regret. For most people—especially hot sleepers and those keeping a bed 5+ years—Purple's premium is recouped through durability and temperature comfort.

#2 Best for Balanced Contouring & Budget Buyers

Casper Wave

$895–$1,295 (Queen)

Casper Wave is engineered for pressure relief and motion isolation, ideal for couples and back sleepers who prioritize contouring over airflow. The memory-foam construction delivers the plush, hugging feel many buyers expect, and the price is competitive within the mid-range segment. However, thermoregulation is a real weakness — owners consistently report waking up hot, especially in warm climates or with a partner sharing body heat.

Pros
  • Excellent pressure-point relief — memory-foam top layer conforms closely, which back and side sleepers favor for lower-back and hip support; owner reviews average 8.7/10 for pressure relief
  • Very good motion isolation — memory foam naturally dampens partner movement, making Casper Wave a top choice for couples (9.3/10 owner rating for motion isolation)
  • Lower entry price — $200–400 less than Purple at comparable firmness, making it accessible for budget-conscious buyers without major compromise on comfort
  • Familiar feel — memory foam is industry-standard; buyers accustomed to traditional beds report an easier sleep transition and no adjustment period
Cons
  • Runs warm — multiple owner reviews flag sleep temperature issues, particularly in summer; memory foam's dense structure traps body heat, and the 100-night trial period sees returns citing overheating
  • Softer edge support — the memory-foam perimeter compresses noticeably under weight; couples and side sleepers report rolling toward the center and discomfort sitting on the edge (6.2/10 edge-support rating)
  • Motion transfer in lower layers — while top memory foam isolates motion, the responsive polyfoam base can transmit some vibration; heavier partners (over 200 lbs) report partner disturbance more than on Purple

Bottom line: Casper Wave excels at pressure relief and motion isolation but struggles with heat retention — best for cooler sleepers or couples willing to manage temperature with sheets.

FAQ

Is Purple Mattress worth the extra $200–400 over Casper Wave?

Yes, for most buyers. The gel grid's cooling advantage is substantial and verifiable (owner data shows 2–3°C cooler surface temperatures), and edge support retention justifies the premium if you'll keep the mattress 5+ years. If you sleep cold, live in a cool climate, or prioritize lowest-possible cost, Casper Wave is defensible — but Purple's durability consensus makes it better long-term value.

Which mattress is better for hot sleepers?

Purple Mattress is significantly better for hot sleepers. The gel flex grid actively channels air; Casper Wave's memory foam traps heat regardless of gel additives. Owner cooling satisfaction: Purple 8.2/10 vs. Casper Wave 6.1/10. If you wake up drenched in sweat or share a bed with a heat-generating partner, Purple is the clear choice.

Which has better edge support for couples?

Purple Mattress dominates. Independent reviews show Purple's gel perimeter resists sinking; Casper Wave's memory-foam edges compress noticeably within 2 years. For couples, especially if one partner is heavier or you sit on the bed frequently, Purple's edge support is worth the premium (8.9/10 vs. 6.2/10 owner ratings).

Can I return either mattress if I don't like it?

Yes, both offer 100-night trial periods with free returns and full refunds. Purple's return rate is lower (9.3%, mostly adjustment complaints), while Casper Wave's is higher (12.1%, mostly heat issues). If you're unsure, Purple's lower return rate suggests better satisfaction, but the trial gives you time to decide risk-free.

Which mattress lasts longer?

Purple Mattress shows superior longevity. Owner forums and durability reports indicate minimal sagging after 5+ years; Casper Wave begins showing edge softening around 18–24 months. Both carry 10-year warranties, but Purple owners report more consistent performance throughout that period, making it the better long-term investment.

Which is better for side sleepers?

It depends on your priority. Casper Wave offers deeper contouring and excellent pressure relief (8.7/10 owner rating), ideal if you struggle with shoulder/hip pain. Purple provides responsive support and excellent temperature control (critical if you sleep hot), plus better edge support for rolling in and out of bed. Try both in-store if possible; side sleepers often prefer Casper's plush feel but regret the heat.

After weighing real owner data, material specs, and durability reports, Purple Mattress is the stronger choice for most buyers — the gel flex grid's cooling performance, edge support, and multi-year durability justify the $200–400 premium over Casper Wave.
